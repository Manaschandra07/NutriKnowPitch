Taking the guesswork out of grocery shopping.

Imagine having a highly intelligent, personal assistant in your pocket that instantly analyzes everything you are about to eat. NutriKnow cuts through deceptive food marketing to make getting fit effortless.

01. THE PROBLEM

Getting fit is hard, mostly because the food industry makes it tricky.

Supermarkets are filled with products claiming to be "all-natural" or "sugar-free," but the reality is much darker. The modern food ecosystem is engineered to confuse consumers and hide cheap, inflammatory ingredients.

Time & Friction

People are busy, and reading food labels is confusing. Trying to understand complex ingredients takes too much effort. Because of this, we often just grab the easiest option.

Legal Tricks

Food companies trick us legally. A label saying "Zero Sugar" might hide ingredients like Maltodextrin, which acts just like sugar in your body. It looks healthy, but it's not.

The Health Cost

Eating highly processed foods with bad oils and fake colors adds up. We eat these harmful ingredients every day without realizing it, simply because the packaging makes it look safe.

NutriKnow Architecture

NutriKnow is a fast, easy-to-use application. It uses your camera to identify products, searches databases to understand the ingredients, and does the hard work of figuring out if a food is good for you. Here is exactly how it works from the moment you scan an item:

1. The Scan

You point your camera at the product's barcode. The application instantly reads the numbers using your phone's built-in tools.

2. Fetching the Data

Once it reads the barcode, the app connects to the Open Food Facts database. It searches for that specific barcode and downloads the product's raw nutrition facts and ingredient list.

3. The Intelligence Engine

This is where the magic happens. Our system looks closely at the ingredients to spot hidden sugars, bad oils, or harmful chemicals. It adds points for healthy things like protein and fiber, and takes points away for bad additives or processed ingredients.

4. Displaying the Result

Finally, the app shows you a simple score from 1 to 100 on your screen. It also gives you an easy-to-read breakdown of exactly why the product got that score, so you know exactly what you are eating.
